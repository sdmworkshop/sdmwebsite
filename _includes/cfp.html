Call for papers

We welcome submissions of short and full papers, and as well as work-in-progress research studies.

In general, we encourage three types of submissions:

  • Technical short paper: Submissions presenting critical and thought-provoking topics within the scientific community.
  • Technical full paper: Submissions presenting validation studies demonstrating clinical relevance on methodological developments and novel applications of artificial intelligence algorithms in Medicine.
  • Research paper: Contributions in this category should focus on novel methodological ideas or theoretical questions without extensive experiments and validation.
Potential topics include:
  • Scalability challenges in generative synthetic healthcare data
  • Trustworthiness and fidelity concerns of synthetic data in HealthCare
  • Applications and computational modeling of multi-modal (e.g., imaging, EMR data, and descriptive text) Generative AI in Healthcare
  • Medical Small and Large Language Models
  • Synthetic data generation enhanced by foundational models for HealthCare
  • Reliability and explainability of synthetic data models in HealthCare
Important Dates

  • Submission deadline: August 30, 2024
  • Notification of acceptance: September 30, 2024
  • Camera-ready papers due: October 10, 2024
  • Workshop date: December 14 or December 15, 2024
  • All deadlines are AoE time.

Submission Guidelines

The SDM 2024 workshop will use OpenReview as the review platform.

Accepted papers will be presented as posters, and a subset of them will be selected for oral presentation. We plan to organize the SDM workshop at NeurIPS 2024 only in-person format. To support the remote presentations, we plan to use Teams platform for the panel and attendees.

The paper template and style files can be found at here (NIPS template). The length of paper can be 2 pages maximun for the research paper, 4 pages maximum for the short paper, and 8 pages maximun for the full paper (excluding references and appendix). Submissions must follow the template and style and should be properly anonymized.

Dual Submission Policy

We welcome papers that have never been submitted. Accepted papers will be published on the workshop homepage.
