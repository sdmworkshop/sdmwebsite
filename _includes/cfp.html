Call for papers

We welcome submissions of short and full papers, and as well as work-in-progress research studies.

In general, we encourage three types of submissions:

  • Technical short paper: Submissions presenting critical and thought-provoking topics within the scientific community.
  • Technical full paper: Submissions presenting validation studies demonstrating clinical relevance on methodological developments and novel applications of artificial intelligence algorithms in Medicine.
  • Research paper: Contributions in this category should focus on novel methodological ideas or theoretical questions without extensive experiments and validation.
Potential topics include:
  • General Deep Learning (deep supervised learning, semi- and self-supervised learning, learning with noisy labels and limited data, etc.)
  • Machine Generative Learning (architectures, generative models, deep reinforcement learning, etc.)
  • Trustworthy Artificial Intelligence (Interpretability and explainable, fair, robust, private, and transparent deep learning)
  • General Medical Artificial Intelligence (Foundation models, Multimodal Data, Medical Large Language models, Integration of imaging and clinical data, etc.)
Important Dates

  • Submission deadline: August 30, 2024
  • Notification of acceptance: September 30, 2024
  • Camera-ready papers due: October 10, 2024
  • Workshop date: December 14 or December 15, 2024
  • All deadlines are AoE time.

Submission Guidelines

The SDM 2024 workshop will use OpenReview as the review platform.

Accepted papers will be presented as posters, and a subset of them will be selected for oral presentation. We plan to organize the SDM workshop at ICML 2024 in a hybrid format. For virtual workshop attendees, we plan to use Teams for the talks/panel and gather for posters/socializing. To support the hybrid format, we will hold parallel meet-and-greet sessions online and in-person.

The paper template and style files can be found at here (ICML template). The length of paper can be 2 pages maximun for the research paper, 4 pages maximum for the short paper, and 8 pages maximun for the full paper (excluding references and appendix). Submissions must follow the template and style and should be properly anonymized.

Dual Submission Policy

We welcome papers that have never been submitted. Accepted papers will be published on the workshop homepage.
